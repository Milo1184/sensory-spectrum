The Garden State's Homegrown Treasure

January 30, 2011

New Jersey is known as "The Garden State," but few people outside of the area know why. The answer can be seen in the pictures below - NJ overflows with farms, growing some of the world's best produce. Go to any town during the summer in NJ, theres a good bet that a Farmer's Market has sprouted up at a municipal building or parking lot downtown. Local farmers drive from all across NJ to these markets to sell produce, meat, cheese, pickles, honey, you name it. The foods are usually straight-from-the- garden fresh and brimming with the flavors of late spring, summer, and early fall. It's also a great place to speak with the farmers directly, to pick their brains for recipes and cooking tips.

While it is not always possible to stop at a market on your way home from work or on a Saturday morning, everyone should try to go at least once during the summer, just to taste something new, haggle with a vendor for a quart of sugar plums, or try an artisanal cheese or salumi. You never know what you may find at a NJ Farmer's Market. You might even discover a new food you enjoy, something you didn't even know existed before your visit.
